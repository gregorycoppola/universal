# Reading List

A recommended reading order for someone new to these ideas, organized from most
accessible to most technical.

## Start Here (Accessible)

1. **Sutton, "The Bitter Lesson" (2019)** — One page. Read it first. It frames the
   entire project as a response.
   http://www.incompleteideas.net/IncIdeas/BitterLesson.html

2. **Pearl and Mackenzie, *The Book of Why* (2018)** — Popular-level introduction
   to causal reasoning and Bayesian networks. No math required.

3. **Pinker, *The Language Instinct* (1994)** — Accessible introduction to
   Chomsky's program and the idea that language has innate structure.

4. **Plato, *Republic* Books VI-VII** — The divided line and the allegory of the
   cave. The oldest statement of the problem this project addresses.

## Core Background (Moderate)

5. **Leibniz, selections on the *characteristica universalis*** — The dream that
   the QBBN realizes. See Jolley (ed.), *The Cambridge Companion to Leibniz*.

6. **Chomsky, *Syntactic Structures* (1957)** — Short and revolutionary. The
   argument that language has structure beyond statistical patterns.

7. **Montague, "The Proper Treatment of Quantification in Ordinary English"
   (1973)** — The paper that connected natural language to formal logic. Technical
   but foundational.

8. **Pearl, *Probabilistic Reasoning in Intelligent Systems* (1988)** — The
   textbook on Bayesian networks and belief propagation. Chapters 1-4 for the
   core ideas.

## The QBBN Papers (Essential)

9. **Coppola, "The Quantified Boolean Bayesian Network" (2024)** — The original
   QBBN paper. AND and OR factors, belief propagation on logical structures.
   arXiv:2402.06557

10. **Coppola, "Statistical Parsing for Logical Information Retrieval" (2026)** —
    The paper this collection accompanies. NEG factors, the typed logical language,
    the grammar, and the LLM-assisted parsing architecture.

## Deep Dives (Technical)

11. **Prawitz, *Natural Deduction* (1965)** — The proof-theoretic foundation.
    Chapters I-IV for the forward fragment, Chapter V for second-order logic.

12. **Koller and Friedman, *Probabilistic Graphical Models* (2009)** — The
    definitive textbook on factor graphs and message passing.

13. **Steedman, *The Syntactic Process* (2000)** — Combinatory Categorial Grammar.
    Contrast with the QBBN's simpler slot grammar.

14. **Richardson and Domingos, "Markov Logic Networks" (2006)** — The closest
    prior work to the QBBN in the probabilistic logic tradition.

## Philosophy Deep Dives (For Context)

15. **Frege, *Begriffsschrift* (1879)** — The invention of predicate logic.

16. **Aristotle, *Prior Analytics*** — The invention of formal logic.

17. **Kowalski, "Predicate Logic as a Programming Language" (1974)** — The
    foundational equation: Algorithm = Logic + Control.

## The LLM Context

18. **Karpathy, "Vibe Coding" (2025)** — The tweet that named the methodology.

19. **Blevins, Gonen, and Zettlemoyer, "Prompting Language Models for Linguistic
    Structure" (2023)** — Evidence that LLMs have metalinguistic knowledge.

20. **Ettinger et al., "You Are an Expert Linguistic Annotator" (2023)** —
    Evidence that LLMs cannot produce exact semantic parses.
